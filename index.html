The Charisma Quadrant

Four types.
One map.

Charisma is the product of two things: Confidence and Social Skills. Where you sit on each axis determines your type — and what to work on first.

01
Higher Confidence · Lower Social Skills
The Occasionally Overconfident

Sharp, capable, and sure of it. You get things done. But confidence without empathy can leave a trail of friction behind it. The missing piece isn’t skill — it’s connection.

02
Higher Confidence · Higher Social Skills
The Courteously Charismatic

Competent and connected. People follow you and you know why. The challenge now is keeping it real as the stakes rise — and not burning out by trying to be everything to everyone.

03
Lower Confidence · Lower Social Skills
The Incidentally Invisible

Talented but overlooked. Your work speaks for itself, but too quietly. The gap isn’t ability — it’s visibility. The moment you get comfortable being seen, everything changes.

04
Lower Confidence · Higher Social Skills
The Unknowingly Influential

You connect naturally, build trust easily, and are often the person others gravitate towards. The only thing underselling you is you.

The Book

Most professionals are more impressive
than they appear

The gap isn’t competence — it’s charisma. And charisma, despite what most people assume, is not a personality trait you either have or lack. It is a measurable skill with identifiable components that can be learned, practised, and made your own.

Open-Source Charisma is built around the Charisma Quadrant — a model that maps every communicator across two dimensions and places them in one of four types. The book then gives you 125 small, high-impact actions organised around your type. Not a transformation programme. Not a personality overhaul.

A map, and the moves that matter most from where you are standing right now.
